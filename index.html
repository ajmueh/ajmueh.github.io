Introduction

I'm a plant ecophysiologist interested in the forms and tradeoffs related to plant adaptation to environmental stress, namely drought and herbivory. These pressures shape species distributions, performance, and dynamics - I'd like to understand how, at all scales from the individual to the landscape. To answer these questions, I use detailed physiological experiments, community-level studies, landscape-level remote sensing, and analytical models.

More broadly, I'm invested in teaching and scientific literacy. General ecology, the ecology of tropical forests, tree physiology, and pest/pathogen dynamics are my specialty. I've also taught and mentored significantly on data/statistical analysis in the programming language R. My goal is to make science relevant, relatable, and actionable to anyone interested.
